During the UBCO Girls in Tech Hackathon, I worked on a multi-major team to build Common Counsel, a platform designed to simplify the legal hurdless of starting a small business. In 48 hours, we developed a scanning engine that parses federal, provincial, and municipal requirements to generate a custom filing timeline and cost estimate for the user. I focused on implementing the AI logic to help entrepreneurs find relevant grants and bursaries, turning a complicated bureaucratic process into a clear, accessible roadmap. This project was a great way to bridge technical web development with real-world problem-solving, resulting in a high-fidelity prototype that makes business ownership more achievable.
